# Maqam — Vision & Strategy

## The One-Line Pitch

Every Muslim needs 5–7 apps to live their deen. Maqam replaces all of them with one that
actually feels good to use.

---

## The Problem

The Muslim app market is broken in a specific way. The apps that exist are either:

- **Functional but ugly** — cheap green gradients, crescent clip-art, slow, no design
  philosophy
- **Polished but hollow** — prayer time widget with nothing else
- **Comprehensive but dishonest** — Muslim Pro (30M+ users) ran ads during prayer times
  and was investigated for selling user location data to the US military

Muslims deserve better. They're using 5–7 apps at once — one for prayer times, one for
Quran, one for Qibla, one for adhkar, one for halal food — and none of them talk to each
other. None of them feel like they were made with love.

---

## What Maqam Is

Maqam is the Muslim life OS. Not a utility. An OS.

It tracks your day around the five pillars:

- When to pray (and whether you did)
- Which direction to face
- What to say after
- What to read
- Where to go

And it does all of it in one place, offline, without ads, without selling your data, with a
design that feels like Apple built it for Muslims.

**The word مقام** (maqam) means both spiritual station — a rank earned through devotion — and
a musical scale, a daily rhythm. The name holds both meanings: a place you aspire toward, a
pattern you return to.

## The Competition

### Muslim Pro

- **30M+ users**, biggest in the market
- **Fatal flaws**: sold user location data, ads during Salah, bloated, slow
- **Our angle**: every Muslim who finds out about the data issue is a potential convert

### Athan / Athan Pro

- Clean prayer time app, good iOS design
- Limited scope — doesn't unify the experience
- **Our angle**: add depth they don't have (offline Quran, Tasbih, community)

### Quran.com

- Best Quran reading experience on web
- Single-purpose, no prayer/qibla integration
- **Our angle**: full Quran reader already shipping in Maqam, improving every sprint

### Al-Moazin / others

- Regional focus, inconsistent quality
- **Our angle**: consistent design and quality bar across all features

## How We Win

### 1. Privacy Is a Feature

- Prayer calculation: 100% client-side. Never hits a server.
- Location: used for calculation, never stored, never sent.
- We say it clearly, repeatedly. Muslims are wary of this. We earn trust.

### 2. Zero Ads During Prayer

- This is a moral line, not a business compromise.
- We monetize through Premium and B2B Masjid Dashboards — not attention.
- Muslim Pro lost users over this. We make the opposite promise, publicly.

### 3. Offline-First, Always

- Works in the masjid basement with no signal.
- Works on a plane crossing timezones.
- Works in rural areas with spotty data.
- Service worker caches everything. No spinner of shame before Fajr.

### 4. Design Like It Matters

- Not another green and gold gradient.
- Obsidian base. Warm amber accents. Lapis for action.
- Arabic typography is primary, not an afterthought.
- Every screen has a clear visual hierarchy. Every interaction has a response.
- Feels like iOS without being a clone.

### 5. Respect for the Act of Worship

- No gamification that cheapens the deen.
- No streak-shaming ("you missed 3 days!").
- No notifications during Salah time (configurable, opt-in only).
- The app should feel like a tool for contemplation, not a Skinner box.

### 6. Unified Experience

- Open the app, know your next prayer time, Hijri date, and current juz — all on one screen.
- Jump to Quran from the prayer times screen.
- Tasbih count feeds back into your daily dhikr log.
- Everything talks to everything.

### 7. Speed as Respect

- Sub-100ms tap response.
- Page turns feel instant.
- No loading skeletons where they're not needed.
- The app should never make you wait.

## Target User

**Primary**: Practicing Muslim, 18–40, smartphone-native, uses English or Arabic, privacy-
conscious, frustrated with existing apps.

**Secondary**: Muslim converts who need guidance on what to do and when.

**Not primary (yet)**: Children's Islamic education, Islamic finance, marriage apps — those
are adjacent products, not Maqam's lane for now.

---

## North Star Metric

**Daily active users who open Maqam for at least 3 of the 5 prayer times.**

If someone opens Maqam at Fajr, Dhuhr, and Isha — we've replaced their fragmented
workflow. That's the win.

Secondary metrics:

- Quran pages read per DAU
- Tasbih sessions per DAU
- Retention D7, D30
- PWA install rate

---

## Brand Principles

1. **Purposeful** — every feature earns its place or gets cut
2. **Honest** — transparent about how data works, how calculation works
3. **Calm** — the app should reduce anxiety, not create it
4. **Beautiful** — Arabic calligraphy is one of humanity's great art forms; treat it that way
5. **Fast** — slowness is disrespect of the user's time

---

## Competitive Moats (long-term)

1. **Masjid network** — if masajid use Maqam dashboards, their congregants use Maqam
2. **Data quality** — community-verified prayer times, halal listings, event calendars
3. **Brand loyalty** — the first app that treats Muslims with respect will be forgiven for bugs
4. **Offline corpus** — full Quran, translations, hadith, adhkar — cached on device is rare
5. **Design language** — hard to copy culture and taste
